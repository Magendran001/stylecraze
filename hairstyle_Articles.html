How To Do A Top Knot – 10 Effortless DIY Top Knot Tutorials

March 3, 2022 Written by Anjali Sayee

The topknot is the poison of choice for many women! You can be a mom, housewife, businesswoman, fitness instructor, chef, or anything else; the topknot is the most convenient and stylish hairstyle. So stick around if you want to learn how to do a topknot. Thanks to the invention of social media, many hairstyles are trending like the topknot. But did you know this messy yet chic hairstyle has been around for a long time? Apart from being a sign of spiritual leadership, the top knot can be seen in many eras and cultures. In South China, married women sported a bun to signify their marital status. To ensure uniformity in ballet, dancers had to wear a bun atop their head which is carried on even now. The Regency Era in England, around the 1800s, boasts of a trendsetting high-bun paired with tight curls or braids. During the Victorian era, the loose Gibson Girl bun was a sign of feminine beauty. So how can you achieve a hairstyle that has captured the hearts of so many decades? Scroll down to learn!

10 Easy DIY Top Knot Tutorials

1. Loose Messy Top Knot

What You Need

  • Dry shampoo
  • Brush
  • Elastic band

How To Do

  1. Brush down all your hair and apply some dry shampoo to it. This will give it texture and volume.
  2. Gather all your hair at the crown to create a high ponytail.
  3. Take an elastic band and pass it over the ponytail once. Twist it and pass it over your hair again, but this time, pass it only halfway through and leave the ends of your hair out.
  4. Loosen up your hair everywhere to make the hairstyle a little bit messy. You can also pancake the bun to make it flair out. Pin down any loose strands of hair to finish off the look.

2. Messy Half Top Knot

What You Need

  • Dry shampoo
  • Brush
  • Bobby pins

How To Do

  1. Start off with unwashed hair for this hairstyle as it will give your top knot some texture.
  2. Brush your hair thoroughly to remove all knots and tangles.
  3. Apply some dry shampoo to hair to give it texture and volume.
  4. Tease your hair at the crown to make it voluminous.
  5. Gather the hair at the top and front of your head. Take an elastic band and pass it over the hair once. Twist the band and pass it a little more than halfway over the hair again. Leave only the ends of your hair out of the bun.
  6. Pancake the bun to give it a messy look.
  7. Secure the ends with bobby pins.

3. 5-Minute Perfect Bun

What You Need

  • Two elastic bands

How To Do

  1. Tie all your hair in a high ponytail.
  2. Lift the hair from the ponytail and twist it once at the base. Wrap the rest of the hair around the twist.
  3. Twist the ends of your hair and wrap them around the base of your bun.
  4. Secure the bun with another elastic band.

4. Easy Top Knot

What You Need

  • Hair brush
  • Elastic band
  • Bobby pins

How To Do

  1. Gather all your hair in a ponytail and smooth out any bumps with your hair brush.
  2. Take an elastic band and pass it through your hair once.
  3. Twist the band, but this time, don’t pass all your hair through. Pass only half of your hair through it.
  4. Loosen up your hair everywhere to make the hairstyle a little bit messy. You can also pancake the bun to make it flair out. Pin down any loose strands of hair to finish off the look.
  5. Stretch the fan bun and wrap the ends of the ponytail around its base. Secure it with bobby pins.
  6. Loosen up the bun to give it a slightly messy look.
  7. Pin down the ends of the fan bun to the sides as well to give it a fuller look.
